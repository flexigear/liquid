# Reference Games

## Mercury Series

Most relevant mainstream reference for controlling a liquid-like mass through motion and inertia.

What to take:

- Motion-based puzzle readability.
- Volume growth through merging.
- Clear level progression through container-like spaces.

What not to inherit:

- Liquid metal ball feel.
- Overly abstract "blob as marble" presentation.

## Vessel

Useful as a reference for making fluid behavior central to the game fantasy, even though the player is not simply swishing water in a glass vessel.

What to take:

- Fluid behavior as the feature, not decoration.
- Technical ambition can be part of product identity.

## Puddle And Fluidity

Less useful for 3D container presentation, but important for proving that direct liquid manipulation can sustain a full game.

What to take:

- Readable puzzle language built around fluid states and volume.
- The liquid itself can be the core object of play.

## From Dust

Relevant for systemic natural-material behavior in 3D rather than for direct one-to-one feature copying.

What to take:

- Material flow can carry spectacle and identity.

## Summary

Closest directional reference is `Mercury`, but the project should diverge toward water-in-glass authenticity rather than metallic blob traversal.
