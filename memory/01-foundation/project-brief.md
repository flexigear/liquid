# Project Brief

## Working Premise

Build a game centered on physically credible liquid motion inside transparent 3D containers.

## Core Fantasy

Recreate the childhood sensation of holding a bottle or glass container, tilting and swirling it, and watching the water lag, climb, split, merge, and settle.

## Core Player Action

The player manipulates the container, not the liquid directly.

## Non-Negotiables

- The liquid should feel materially closer to water in glass than to a rolling ball or stylized slime.
- The container is a meaningful part of gameplay and presentation, not a background prop.
- Visible inertia, wall interaction, and merging behavior are essential.
- The game should prefer 3D containers and transparent materials.

## Constraints

- Physical authenticity is a priority even when it makes implementation harder.
- Some control-side stabilization is acceptable if it preserves the illusion of real liquid in a real container.
- Early work should focus on one highly convincing prototype before broader level content.

## Non-Goals

- Do not optimize early for arcade speed or exaggerated "liquid metal marble" behavior.
- Do not reduce the liquid to rigid spheres unless only used as a temporary debug approximation.
- Do not design around many disconnected gimmicks before the core bottle-water feel is proven.

## Success Test

If a player says "this feels like swishing water around inside a glass container," the project direction is working.
