# MVP: Square Glass Container

## Scope

Build a single convincing interaction prototype before adding progression systems.

## Scene Contents

- One transparent square glass container
- 8 to 20 initial water drops
- Basic lighting sufficient to read glass and liquid
- No scoring, no level sequence, no auxiliary mechanics

## Player Input

The player rotates the container using a trackball-style control.

## Required Behaviors

- Drops slide along the inner walls.
- Drops collide with glass and visibly deform.
- Nearby drops merge into larger drops.
- Stopping input does not stop the liquid immediately; the fluid settles over time.

## Success Criteria

- The result feels closer to swishing water in a container than moving rigid blobs.
- Merging is easy to read.
- Wall contact makes the liquid feel attached to glass instead of floating.
- The prototype is stable enough to iterate on parameters quickly.

## Explicitly Deferred

- Progression systems
- Multiple vessel types
- Puzzle objectives
- Valves and special mechanics
- Heavy cinematic water rendering
